Dear Colleague,
 
As part of our effort to provide greater flexibility for future authentication and security enhancements to Office 365, we need to align Skype for Business account locations with
 the Exchange mailbox locations.  Skype for Business accounts are now migrated as part of a department’s email migration, but that did not happen with our early adopter participants, including your department.

 
What to expect
 
You are receiving this message because we plan to move your Skype for Business account to Office 365.  We plan to begin moving accounts on Tuesday, May 28th at 9:00 am.  We estimate that it could take up to three days to move
 all affected accounts.  You should not see any Skype service outages during the change, but you may be prompted to login again when your move completes.

 
Anticipated impact

 


Limitation of client selection:  Prior to this move, employees could choose to access Skype from many supported clients, including open source tools.  Once the account is moved to Office 365, Microsoft may begin restricting Skype access to only supported
 clients along with basic IM functionality found in OWA (Heelmail).  Most managed devices within ITS should have a supported client. 

Prompt for login:  When the move completes you may be prompted to login again.  For employees using the latest versions of Skype for Business for MAC and PC, this should be the only impact.

The list of
supported desktop clients and their supported capabilities can be found here:

 
https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dn933896.aspx .  

 
The list of
supported mobile clients and their supported capabilities can be found here:
